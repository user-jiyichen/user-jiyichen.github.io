A (very) personalized list of writings I found inspiring and worth returning to.

**Philosophy**
- Johnny Reb, [Constructive Skepticism, Critical Thinking and the Ethics of Belief](https://www.skeptic.ca/Constructive_Skepticism.htm)  
“The constructive skeptic is a stoical optimist who has the capacity to laugh at failures and disappointments... The constructive skeptic is also critically aware of authority, in a world in which one is increasingly dependent on experts and specialists rather than direct personal experience and thinking for oneself  (perhaps real personal experience is becoming obsolete).”

- Baihua Zong, [An Aesthetics Anthology](https://www.taylorfrancis.com/books/mono/10.4324/9781003368533/aesthetics-anthology-zong-baihua)


**Blogs**
- Dan Mackinlay, [Blogs on Bayes](https://danmackinlay.name/#category=Bayes)
Insightful collections on various topics.

- Paul Graham, [How to do great work](https://paulgraham.com/greatwork.html)

- Richard Hamming, [You and Your Research](https://www.cs.virginia.edu/~robins/YouAndYourResearch.html)

- Eugene Vinitsky, [Personal Rules of Productive Research](https://www.eugenevinitsky.com/posts/personal-rules-of-productive-research/)

**Texts**
- John Duchi, [Statistics and Information Theory](https://web.stanford.edu/class/ee377/lecture-notes.pdf)
